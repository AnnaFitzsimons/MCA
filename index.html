Week 1: Musical Datasets

Current manifestations of this dataset theme include a "Broadway" Genre on Spotify. This contains playlists such as:

  • Best of Broadway
  • Sucessos da Broadway
  • Hits from Musicals
  • Ballads from Musicals

The app also possesses several unofficial playlists made by spotify users


How is the data presented, described and analysed?

The data is presented in a series of playlists, each accompanied by a cover photo as well as the number of followers the playlist has. Compared to other genres "Broadway" has significantly less content within it, suggesting a lack of demand. Each song that exists within the playlist comes with 'Song Credits' which includes who the song is 'Performed by'; 'Written by'; 'Produced by'; as well as the 'Source' of the music.
Spotify presumably produces these genre playlists by analysing each song's accompanying metadata.

What information has been lost?

The new composition appears to be completely all over the place and not at all resembling the original. The time signature has changed from a duple to a triple which has resulted in a change in tempo. Additionally the piece has changed from an A major sharp to a F major flat.
Does removing the "voice" instrument make any difference?
From this I noticed no notable differences.

Overall, the new composition seems to have lost the majority of its' structure and seems to have added bars as it has increased from 102 in the original composition; to 135 in the transcribed version. In addition the new composition fails to provide any information regarding what instruments are being used. Upon listening to the new track I was able to distinguish the similarities to the original piece however in general it is rather haphazard.
